Daniel Sieg is shaping the future of hand therapy with his bachelor thesis at LIME. In the interview, he talks about the dynamics of the startup and how it continues to work for him now.
In 2018, Daniel was about to finish his B.Sc. Physical Engineering at the University of Applied Sciences RheinMain and was looking for a company in which he could take the final step to graduation, the bachelor thesis. His focus is on the development of medical technology, so Daniel has looked on the homepage of the University of Mainz . There he came across LIME.

Before you apply for a job, you look at yourself: Who are the bosses? What is behind it? And with such a young startup, where the founders are younger than me, these questions were not without. I just applied once and when I was there, I immediately realized that there was a lot of knowledge and competence to write my bachelor thesis here.

At the job interview Daniel was directly challenged: "That was different than I am used to! Every half hour, I got new tasks and then the founders and I brainstormed together. "He mastered this special kind of job interview well and the actually advertised job vacancy was converted into a thesis.
It was not a problem that there were no graduate engineers available to take care of the work. The university provided Daniel with two professors, with whom he discussed the state of development at longer intervals. At Weekly, the weekly meeting, he consults with the founders. The good relationship in the team has contributed enormously to the quality of these discussions. "In LIME, the hierarchy does not count. If you give a good opinion and represent why you find something particularly important, idea goes into position. "
What is the difference between working in a large company or in a young startup? "You have to be open to the fact that many processes are still in the identification phase and processes are constantly being optimized. It is tried and ground. Of course, since my professors were not there, I often had to look for information myself and build up my own knowledge. This self-sustained problem solving takes longer, but the learning effect is much greater. "The standards for the development of a medical device Daniel were already known from his studies, the practical application now followed in his work. "In other companies whole departments deal with this - and I might not have come into contact with this task at all."
In addition, Daniel was able to work on a very extensive topic at LIME, which goes far beyond the mere execution of a series of experiments. It requires self-initiative and you have to find out for yourself what the next meaningful step looks like. After the work does not disappear in a drawer or a bookshelf, but serves to implement a therapy device. It will cover another phase of hand therapy after the patient has already made progress in range of motion through training with LIME's AnyHand. The practical implementation and the added value for the hand therapy have motivated Daniel especially:

Conversion and sales reports can help you measure how effective displays are at attracting shoppers, and convincing them to make a purchase.

The expertise in hand therapy has received Daniel, inter alia, by questioning of occupational therapists. This input from practice, eg. B. by Anette Philipp makes it possible to find a tailor-made solution for everyday therapy.

Recently, Daniel has also learned the grades of his bachelor thesis: In the written part he can look forward to a smooth 1.0 and achieved a 1.3 in the oral defense - at this point, congratulations on this great achievement! In addition, we are pleased that he will remain in the team for the long term - Daniel will continue to manage his project and develop it to market maturity and will now also take care of the QM system.
"Exciting, independent and meaningful" - so he would summarize the work at LIME on three attributes. And he also recommends that other graduates apply if they are looking for a demanding topic in which they can contribute their own ideas to a high degree. "Of course, the whole thing went well - we're a cool team of young people who enjoy themselves and are happy to come to work every morning."
